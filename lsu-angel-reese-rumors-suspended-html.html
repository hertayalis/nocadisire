LSU women's basketball star Angel Reese hits out at rumors she's been suspended from the Tigers, ins

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Monday, August 5, 2024

Angel Reese, one of the biggest stars in women's college basketball, responded to rumors on social media that she's been suspended, urging fans not to believe the information that's circulating. 

'Please don’t believe everything you read,' Reese said on X Sunday in response to the rumors claiming that she's been suspended from No. 7 LSU for having a low grade point average. 

Reese didn't play in Friday's game against Southeastern Louisiana.

LSU head coach Kim Mulkey said that Reese 'is a part of this basketball team, and we hope she’s back with the team soon,' when pressed about the 21-year-old's absence.

Mulkey refused to expand on her answer any further.

Reese's status going forward is unknown and she last played Tuesday night against Kent State

LSU's Kim Mulkey says Reese is still with the team, but didn't reveal any other information

Reese was benched during the second half of the Tigers game on Tuesday against Kent State, which Mulkey called a coach's decision.

According to Outkick's Glen Guilbeau, a source close to the team said Mulkey is benching Reese to try and adjust the junior's 'attitude' problem, which appeared to be supported by an online exchange between her mom, Angel Webb Reese, and Kia Brooks, mother of Reese's sophomore teammate Flau'jae Johnson.

The Tigers return to action against Texas Southern on Monday night. Reese's availability for that game is unknown.  

Reese was an integral part of LSU's national championship run last season, bringing Mulkey a championship in her second season as the team's head coach.

The Tigers are 4-1 this season after suffering a season-opening loss to No. 5 Colorado. 

Reese is averaging a double-double this season with 17 points and 10.3 rebounds per game.
